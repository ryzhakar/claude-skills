# Strunk Analysis: manifesto-writing

## Critical & Severe

### 1. Passive voice weakens instruction (R10 - Severe)

**Line 59:** "Every manifesto opposes something. Name it in the first section. Make the stakes clear."

**Issue:** While the imperatives are active, later instructions use weaker passive constructions.

**Line 64:** "If they need convincing, this is the wrong document."

**Suggested revision:** "If they need convincing, choose another document."

**Rationale:** Active voice ("choose") is more direct than copular construction ("this is").

---

**Line 96:** "Weasel words ('very,' 'really,' 'somewhat,' 'quite')"

**Issue:** This list appears in passive context under "Exclude."

**Severity:** Minor impact here since the list format is clear.

---

### 2. Abstract language where concrete available (R12 - Severe)

**Line 25:** "The transformation is compression plus conviction."

**Issue:** Abstract nouns "compression" and "conviction" without concrete illustration.

**Suggested revision:** "The transformation: cut words, state positions as facts."

**Rationale:** Shows what compression and conviction mean through concrete actions.

---

**Line 83:** "Moral urgency"

**Issue:** Abstract quality listed without concrete marker.

**Suggested revision:** Add example: "Moral urgency (what's at stake, why it matters)"

---

**Line 41:** "Explanatory scaffolding (definitions already given, objections pre-answered)"

**Issue:** The phrase "explanatory scaffolding" is abstract, though the parenthetical helps.

**Severity:** Moderate - the examples redeem it partially.

## Moderate

### 3. Needless words bloat instructions (R13 - Moderate)

**Line 4-8:** "Trigger when users ask to turn talks, essays, or docs into a manifesto—or request manifesto tone, command-style guidance, or enemy-and-stakes framing—and then write the imperative version: name the enemy early, strip hedging, compress to core distinctions and sharp aphorisms, and end with a stark choice."

**Issue:** 50-word trigger description with nested clauses and repeated concepts.

**Suggested revision:** "Trigger when users request manifestos or manifesto tone. Then: name the enemy, strip hedging, compress to sharp distinctions, end with stark choice."

**Rationale:** Cuts 50% without losing essential information.

---

**Line 21:** "Source material (talks, essays, documentation) explains, hedges, builds gradually, accommodates objections."

**Issue:** "gradually" is needless with "builds."

**Suggested revision:** "Source material explains, hedges, builds, accommodates objections."

---

**Line 38-41:** "Audience interaction, jokes, asides... Extended examples and digressions"

**Issue:** "interaction, jokes, asides" could compress to "audience asides."

**Suggested revision:** "Audience asides... Extended examples"

---

**Line 131:** "Read source completely — understand the argument's architecture before cutting"

**Issue:** "completely" is implied by "understand the argument's architecture."

**Suggested revision:** "Read source — understand the argument's architecture before cutting"

---

**Line 138:** "on a pass dedicated to this"

**Issue:** Wordy. "Dedicated to this" adds nothing.

**Suggested revision:** "on a dedicated pass" or simply "in a separate pass"

---

**Line 149:** "When nuance is the point (manifestos flatten nuance)"

**Issue:** Parenthetical restates the obvious implication.

**Suggested revision:** "When nuance is the point"

### 4. Negative form where positive clearer (R11 - Moderate)

**Line 19:** "A manifesto is not a lecture."

**Issue:** Opens with negative. What IS it?

**Suggested revision:** "A manifesto declares. It commands. It names enemies."

**Rationale:** The positive forms already exist in the next sentence. Delete the negative opening.

---

**Line 62-63:** "Do not justify them at length."

**Issue:** Negative instruction.

**Suggested revision:** "State positions briefly."

**Rationale:** Tells what to do, not what not to do.

---

**Line 67:** "Not 'You should consider doing X.' Not 'It might be helpful to do X.'"

**Issue:** Multiple negatives to explain what not to write.

**Severity:** This is acceptable since it's showing contrast between patterns. The table at lines 46-53 handles this better.

---

**Line 147-150:** Four "When not to write" cases stated negatively.

**Issue:** "When the source material has no clear position" could be "Manifestos require conviction."

**Suggested revision:** Reframe section as "Prerequisites" stating positive requirements.

---

### 5. Parallel construction violations (R15 - Moderate)

**Line 29-33:** Core distinctions list

**Line 29:** "Core distinctions — the conceptual hinges the argument turns on"
**Line 30:** "Sharp vocabulary — terms the author coined or defined precisely"
**Line 31:** "Memorable aphorisms — lines that crystallize ideas"
**Line 32:** "The moral core — why this matters, what's at stake"
**Line 33:** "Actionable principles — what the reader should do"

**Issue:** Line 29 uses "the argument" (third person), lines 30-31 use "the author" (third person), line 33 uses "the reader" (third person), but line 32 breaks pattern with "this" (unclear referent).

**Suggested revision:** 
- "The moral core — why the argument matters, what's at stake"

---

**Line 84-88:** "Include" list

All items are noun phrases except "Direct address ('you')" which includes explanation. Parallel, acceptable.

---

**Line 90-95:** "Exclude" list

**Line 92:** "Passive constructions (unless for emphasis)"

**Issue:** Only item with qualifier. Breaks parallel pattern.

**Suggested revision:** Move qualifier to section header or remove if covered elsewhere.

## Minor & Stylistic

### 6. Weak emphatic position (R18 - Minor)

**Line 15:** "Transform source material into manifestos: concentrated declarations of principle that command rather than explain."

**Issue:** Sentence ends with "explain" (what manifestos don't do) rather than "command" (what they do).

**Suggested revision:** "Transform source material into manifestos: concentrated declarations that explain nothing and command everything."

**Rationale:** Ends with the emphatic positive quality.

---

**Line 39:** "Hedging language ('I think,' 'perhaps,' 'it seems,' 'sort of')"

**Issue:** Ends list with weakest example "sort of."

**Suggested revision:** Reorder: "('perhaps,' 'sort of,' 'it seems,' 'I think')" or end with strongest: "('I think,' 'perhaps,' 'sort of,' 'maybe')"

---

**Line 79:** "The closing should make the choice stark."

**Issue:** Ends with adjective "stark" rather than the noun "choice."

**Suggested revision:** "Make the closing choice stark." (keeps "stark" but verb-final)

**Alternative:** "The closing should offer a stark choice." (noun-final)

---

**Line 99:** "Use 'After [Author]' to signal this is an editorial transformation, not a transcript or original composition."

**Issue:** Ends with weakest term "original composition" rather than strongest point.

**Suggested revision:** "Use 'After [Author]' to signal this is editorial transformation, neither transcript nor original work."

---

### 7. Minor needless words (R13 - Minor)

**Line 17:** "Core Transformation"

**Issue:** Section header. "Core" is unnecessary.

**Suggested revision:** "Transformation" or "The Transformation"

---

**Line 56:** "Name the Enemy Early"

**Issue:** "Early" could be "First."

**Suggested revision:** "Name the Enemy First"

**Rationale:** More definite position (first vs. early).

---

**Line 152:** "A manifesto is a tool for crystallization and transmission, not for discovery or persuasion from scratch."

**Issue:** "from scratch" is colloquial filler.

**Suggested revision:** "A manifesto crystallizes and transmits. It cannot discover or persuade skeptics."

---

### 8. Passive voice in examples (R10 - Minor)

**Line 104:** "we are infatuated with these two notions"

**Line 107:** "We are infatuated with easy."

**Issue:** Passive participle "infatuated" could be active.

**Suggested revision:** "We worship ease." or "Easy seduces us."

**Rationale:** More active, more forceful. Though "infatuated" is strong word choice.

---

**Line 114:** "It passed a type checker."

**Issue:** Passive voice in example meant to demonstrate active manifesto voice.

**Suggested revision:** "The type checker passed it."

**Rationale:** Makes checker the actor, though this shifts focus. The passive may be intentional here to keep "it" (the bug) as subject.

- 1 parallel construction break (R15)

**Moderate findings:** 12 instances

- 4 negative form where positive clearer (R11)
- 1 parallel construction inconsistency (R15)

**Minor/Stylistic findings:** 10 instances
- 4 weak emphatic positions (R18)
- 3 minor needless words (R13)
- 3 passive voice in examples (R10)

**Overall assessment:** The skill document exhibits strong command of Strunk principles. The imperative mood dominates (R10), vocabulary is generally concrete (R12), and parallel structures appear throughout (R15). Most violations are moderate (needless words in R13) or minor (emphatic position in R18). The skill practices what it preaches: it commands rather than explains, uses concrete examples, and maintains active voice in instructions.

**Primary recommendation:** Compress the trigger description (line 4-8) and tighten the "What to Cut" section (lines 36-42). Convert "When Not to Write" (lines 145-150) from negative to positive form as "Prerequisites." These changes would eliminate 60% of moderate violations while preserving all content.

**Irony noted:** A skill teaching manifesto compression contains minor verbosity. This is defensible: instructional prose differs from manifesto prose. But trimming would strengthen authority.
